Sepsis

Sepsis is a generalized inflammatory response associated with an infection. If it occurs after an invasive procedure, it is called nosocomial infection. Sepsis can be caused by bacteria or certain viruses (SARS-Cov2, H1N1 influenza...). Currently, sepsis is responsible for 60,000 deaths per year in France and represents 1 in 5 deaths in the world. Each hour of delay in diagnosis reduces the patient's chances of survival by 7%.

With a 30-day mortality rate of 20-30%*, sepsis is one of the leading causes of death in hospitals around the world.

Accurate algorithm and real-time alerts reduce length of stay and rate mortality rate significantly.

* The association between four scoring systems and 30-day mortality among intensive care patients with sepsis: a cohort study - Nature, Mai 2021

What is the new technology FHIR ?

FHIR is a standard

HL7 FHIR is the newest international healthcare standard allowing third party apps to securely access EHR data in real time.

FHIR is a community 

FHIR is not only a standard but a large and international community sharing knowledge, tools and code.

FHIR is an ecosystem

Just as the application store on smartphones changed the way companies develop apps and integrate them into a larger environment, FHIR allows emerging app gallery like SMART on FHIR.

FHIR is taking off

The Argonaut Project is an implementation community comprising leading US technology vendors including Accenture, Apple, Cerner, Epic and provider organizations to accelerate the use of FHIR.

FHIR is open

FHIR specification is open source and available on the HL7 FHIR website.

FHIR is secure

Users and clients accessing data must be authenticated. We recommend OPenID Connect and OAuth using the Smart on FHIR profile
